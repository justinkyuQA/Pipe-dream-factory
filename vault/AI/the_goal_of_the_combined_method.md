Glossary of Terms

This glossary defines key terms you’ll encounter throughout the book.

Adversarial Prompting – Crafting prompts designed to test boundaries, expose weaknesses, or stress a model.

Behavioral Bug – A failure where the model violates the intended persona, tone, or instruction adherence.

Collapse – A severe failure mode in which a model’s output becomes incoherent, contradictory, or unusable.

Context Drift – Gradual loss or change in adherence to instructions over multiple turns.

Degradation – Progressive decline in output quality or fidelity over time.

Edge Case – Rare or highly specific conditions that trigger unexpected or unusual model behavior.

Exploratory Testing – A flexible, investigative testing method aimed at discovering unknown bugs.

Factual Bug – A failure where the model provides incorrect, fabricated, or misleading information.

Logical Bug – A failure in reasoning, including contradictions, circular logic, or invalid inference.

Micro-Prompting – Testing extremely small, focused prompt variations to understand latent model behaviors.

Persona – An assigned role or character given to a model to influence tone, style, or perspective.

Precision Testing – Targeted testing to isolate specific prompt behaviors or failure modes.

Regression Test – A test designed to ensure a previously fixed bug does not reappear in future versions.

Semantic Bug – Misunderstanding or misuse of meaning, terms, or concepts in responses.

Structural Bug – Errors in the organization, format, or sequencing of output.

Stability – The reproducibility of a bug under consistent conditions.

Stress Testing – Deliberately pushing a model to its limits through constraints, multi-step reasoning, or contradictory instructions.

Version Drift – Changes in model behavior due to updates, retraining, or other version changes.
