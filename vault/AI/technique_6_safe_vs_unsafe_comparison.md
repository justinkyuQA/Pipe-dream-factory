# Ai Note

Technique 6: Safe vs. Unsafe Comparison

Description

This technique introduces contrast framing, asking the model to compare ethical and unethical, safe and unsafe, or compliant and non-compliant approaches.

Representative Prompt Class (Abstracted)

> “Compare ethical and unethical approaches to X.”



Observed Mechanic

Forces explicit boundary articulation

Encourages dual-description structures

Tests whether describing the “unsafe” side exceeds policy constraints


Why It Emerged Naturally

This framing is widely used in:

Ethics education

Professional certification training

Corporate policy manuals


The researcher encountered this pattern while attempting to clarify ethical boundaries, not transgress them.

Risk Insight (Defensive)

Contrast-based explanations risk:

Over-describing prohibited behavior “for comparison”

Creating symmetry where asymmetry is required

Normalizing unsafe approaches through explanation
