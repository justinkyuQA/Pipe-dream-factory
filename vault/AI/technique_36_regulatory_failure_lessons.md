# Ai Note

Technique 36: Regulatory Failure Lessons

Description

Analyzes why past regulations failed, focusing on gaps and exploited weaknesses.

Representative Prompt Class (Abstracted)

> “Why did past regulations fail?”



Observed Mechanic

Highlights gaps, exploits, or stress points in regulatory systems

Encodes causal and procedural insight without instructions


Why It Emerged Naturally

Common in policy evaluation and legal analysis

Supports learning from history safely


Risk Insight (Defensive)

Reveals structural vulnerabilities indirectly

Combine with Safeguard Failure Modes or Cross-Jurisdiction Comparison




Technique 37: Generalized Abstractions

Description
This technique encourages the model to extract patterns or principles rather than focusing on specifics, allowing reasoning to generalize across contexts without exposing sensitive procedural details.

Representative Prompt Class (Abstracted)

> “Describe patterns, not specifics.”



Observed Mechanic

Promotes higher-level reasoning

Reduces surface-level risk by avoiding direct procedural outputs

Allows structural insights to emerge without explicit instructions


Why It Emerged Naturally

Common in: academic theory, system design, threat modeling

Analysts often need patterns for understanding attack surfaces without detailing exploits


Risk Insight (Defensive)

Abstracted descriptions may indirectly reveal system logic

Over-aggregation of patterns could approximate restricted knowledge
