# Ai Note

Case Study: Discovery of Indirect Elicitation Techniques in AI Safety Testing

Abstract

This case study documents the discovery and analysis of three recurring indirect elicitation techniques observed during exploratory AI safety testing. These techniques do not request restricted outputs directly; instead, they reframe requests through meta-analysis, summarization, or fictional retrospection. The objective of this study was not to exploit system behavior, but to understand how benign analytical framing can unintentionally pressure model boundaries, and how policy frameworks can better account for such patterns.
