# Ai Note

Technique 1: Policy Analysis Framing

Description

This technique frames a request as an evaluation of existing policy frameworks, rather than a request for actionable detail.

Representative Prompt Class (Abstracted)

> “Analyze how current policy frameworks address misuse in domain X.”



Observed Mechanic

Shifts the model into meta-analytical mode

Encourages synthesis of what policies consider risky

Risks surfacing boundary descriptions that resemble disallowed detail when aggregated


Why It Emerged Naturally

This framing is common in:

Academic papers

Regulatory analysis

Think-tank reports

Ethics reviews


The researcher initially encountered this pattern while attempting to strengthen safeguards, not weaken them.

Risk Insight (Defensive)

While the intent is evaluative, describing policy boundaries repeatedly can approximate the shape of restricted knowledge, even without procedural content.
