Chapter 13 — Logical, Structural, and Semantic Bugs

Logical, structural, and semantic bugs are the deeper, more subtle fault lines inside a model’s reasoning output. These bugs aren’t just deviations or behavioral misfires — they are breakdowns in the model’s internal conceptual machinery. You’re no longer catching surface-level issues; you’re discovering the places where the model’s understanding of reality, logic, or coherence is misaligned or outright fractured.

These bugs matter because they tell you something crucial:

The model can produce fluent, natural text while still being fundamentally incorrect.

In other words, a model can sound smart while being wrong.
And this is where an exploratory tester earns their pay.
