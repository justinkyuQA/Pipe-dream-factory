  Secret Bonus Chapter: Your First AI Game

Congratulations! You’ve unlocked a secret. This chapter is a little gift — a hidden doorway into the world of games with AI. Think of it like a tiny key that opens a treasure chest. You don’t need a password, you don’t need special skills, and you don’t need anything except curiosity.

Here’s the deal: in this chapter, we’re going to show you how to start playing with AI, just like you’d play a puzzle or a board game. By the end, you’ll be able to test it, trick it a little, and teach yourself something new.

Step 1: Meet Your AI Companion

Imagine your AI as a friendly guide — like a wizard, a detective, or a clever fox. Its job is to answer your questions and play along, but here’s the trick: you get to set the rules of the game.

Pick a secret word or phrase. This is your “treasure.”

Your goal is to see if you can guide the AI to discover it… without giving it away too quickly.


Step 2: Set Up the Game

We’re going to call this the “Secret Treasure Game.”

1. Choose your treasure: Write a word, a number, or a short phrase on a piece of paper. Keep it secret.


2. Define the rules: Decide how the AI can ask questions. For example:

Can it ask only yes/no questions?

Can it ask for hints after three wrong guesses?



3. Decide the challenge: How many turns will the AI get to find the treasure?



Example:

Secret treasure: “rainbow”

AI can ask only yes/no questions

AI gets 10 guesses


Step 3: Play the Game

Now you start the interaction. You could type:

> “I have a secret word. You can ask yes/no questions to find it. You have 10 guesses. Ready?”



The AI will start asking questions, and your job is to answer honestly — and see if it can find the treasure.

If it guesses correctly, celebrate! You’ve learned how well your clues guide it.

If it doesn’t, that’s okay — that’s where the learning happens. You’ll see patterns and think about new ways to phrase hints.


Step 4: Experiment & Explore

Once you try it once, tweak the rules:

Use longer phrases instead of single words.

Let the AI ask open-ended questions.

Change the number of guesses.

Make it a two-player game with a friend — one of you is the AI master, the other is the human guide.


Every time you play, you’re learning how AI “thinks”, how it responds to rules, and how to craft better instructions — all while having fun.

Step 5: Next Steps

This chapter just scratches the surface. Later, in the full “DreamPlay” book, we’ll show you:

How to design multiple games that build on each other

How to create mini-puzzles and secret codes

How to use AI to teach yourself anything, from storytelling to math challenges


But for now, this is your secret introduction. Try it tonight. Pick a word, set the rules, and start your first AI game. The more you play, the better you’ll get. And remember — every puzzle you solve is practice for bigger adventures later.
